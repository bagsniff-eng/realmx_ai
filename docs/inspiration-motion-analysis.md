## Realm XAI inspiration motion analysis

Frame-by-frame review was done from the uploaded videos in `remotion/public` by extracting frames at 2 fps into `docs/inspiration-analysis/*`.

### Per-video observations

`6C5Ad6-LpAkG4E42.mp4`
- Opens with oversized geometric crop and very sparse copy.
- Quickly moves into a dark section with logos, badges, and icon clusters.
- Uses white-to-black contrast as a pacing reset.
- Product UI appears only after multiple brand/message beats.

`6f_iDDYUQo8qtxrG.mp4`
- Minimal cream background with one dominant interface surface at a time.
- Text enters as typed or staged fragments, not long paragraphs.
- Alternates dark and light scenes for rhythm.
- One UI module is repeated in multiple states rather than showing many unrelated modules.

`7jea32bYEJX8aiPh.mp4`
- Soft pastel background with repeated headline progression.
- Product tiles and cards drift in clusters.
- Constant vertical pacing and modular swaps.
- Relies on repetition and momentum more than dramatic camera moves.

`hN7MVDwzxa031jqv.mp4`
- Heavy use of dark premium lighting and 3D object staging.
- Statistics, globes, radial maps, and stacked dashboards appear in waves.
- Camera keeps reframing the same world rather than cutting to empty scenes.
- Feels like a motion system, not a collection of isolated shots.

`iiztDFg_75uoQyrh.mp4`
- Light blue environment with floating avatars, pills, charts, and micro-panels.
- Headline fragments and UI objects coexist instead of taking turns.
- Good example of “always-on” motion without cluttering every single frame.

`Kr0wTbgIxO2G1hF8.mp4`
- Black cinematic product film language.
- Uses pedestals, spotlight beams, counters, graph lights, and many small HUD elements.
- Strong hierarchy: hero object first, then network, then product proof.
- Several scenes keep 5 or more supporting surfaces alive around the core object.

`nyDC6vMB4unhuw2q.mp4`
- Fast pastel collage and bold typography.
- Uses text fragments, phone stacks, receipts, UI strips, and checkerboard transitions.
- Message refresh is very frequent.
- Strong inspiration for dense multi-panel ad pacing.

`Pl6jpGmvb6yZREoY.mp4`
- Clean white product-intro structure.
- Headline builds word by word while logos and channels appear around it.
- Uses a repeated central bar/input motif.
- Very readable, but still constantly changing.

`uqJ_M_PNHHPt190d.mp4`
- Terminal/install aesthetic, but still stylized and highly composed.
- Good reference for staged typing and “productized terminal” treatment.
- Works because the whole frame is still designed, not plain command output.

`vtbB6d0D0fJfut4R.mp4`
- Dark purple/indigo card choreography with constant object swaps.
- Starts with message fragments and evolves into floating app surfaces.
- Good example of mixed light/dark staging within one ad.

`_nLSxJ4Q3W0t2UGR.mp4`
- Light gradient editorial feel.
- Uses app windows, mail-like UI, buttons, and task bars in layered sequences.
- Multiple interface cards stay on screen together while copy advances.
- Clean and premium despite high scene density.

### Shared motion patterns across the set

- The screen is almost never empty.
- Copy is short and changes often.
- There are usually multiple panels on screen at once.
- Panels slide, rotate, stack, fan out, orbit, or cascade.
- Camera movement is continuous or implied through layout drift.
- Brands appear early and then reappear throughout.
- One visual motif gets repeated across the whole ad.
- Backgrounds are clean, but the compositions are busy with intent.

### What the next Realm XAI cuts must do

- Keep 4 to 10 surfaces active in most scenes.
- Refresh headline/message states every 30 to 60 frames.
- Use stronger panel choreography: cascades, carousels, orbit rings, stacked strips, tiled walls.
- Keep Realm XAI visible throughout, not only at the start or end.
- Use product-authentic message themes:
  - private execution
  - infrastructure of trust
  - selective disclosure
  - identity preservation
  - referrals and participation
  - node dashboard as one module, not the whole product
- Avoid large dead zones, oversized single-card scenes, and long static sections.
